Cities to Travel

Surat

Surat is a large city beside the Tapi River in the west Indian state of Gujarat. Once known for silk weaving, Surat remains a commercial centre for textiles, and the New Textile Market area is lined with fabric shops.

Jaipur

The beautiful Pink city is the capital of Rajasthan, is also called as India’s most mesmerizing state. The city has many traditional shops selling antiques and handicrafts and is also a great place to try out the world-famous Rajasthani cuisine.

Goa

Goa, known for its beaches - ranging from popular stretches at Baga and Palolem to those in laid-back fishing villages such as Agonda. No doubt why Goa is part of the most Instagrammed city in the country! Goan cuisine and food are famous for its rich variety of fish dishes cooked with elaborate recipes.

Instagrammed cities

Much like every year, the review of moments, events and trends from India and abroad on social media that had happened to be big in the year are doing rounds on the internet. In one such exploration, the photo-sharing platform Instagram has reviewed the most Instagrammed cities in India and the list is out.

New Delhi

Capital city of India is considered one of is most vibrant gems. The city of Delhi is a marvellous travel destination for both Indians and foreigners because of its well-preserved historic monuments and modern high-rise buildings.

Mumbai

Known as the financial capital of India, Mumbai perhaps has the busiest city life. Not just that, the city is a delight for visitors. Mumbai has been an intriguing destination for travellers over the years. Its strange contrast of poor slums and chawls go hand in hand with skyscrapers and luxury hotels.

Bengaluru

Bengaluru is a great city that is home to the country’s young professional network. It is also known as India’s Silicon Valley because of its role as the nation's leading information technology (IT) exporter. The center of India's high-tech industry, the city is also known for its parks and nightlife.

Other cities

Hyderabad

The beautiful Charminar is almost synonymous with the city. A major center for the technology industry, it's home to many upscale restaurants and shops and famous for the spicy Hyderabadi biryani. Its colourful people and wonderful heritage, entertains the people who go to visit this beautiful piece of land.

Pune

Pune is known for its pleasant weather and lovely temples. It is also located near some of the finest hill stations and trekking destinations of Maharashtra. Considered to be the cultural capital of Maharashtra, Pune is known as "Oxford of the East" due to the presence of several well-known educational institutions in the city.

Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad is the largest city in the state of Gujarat and has emerged as an important economic and industrial hub in the country. Ahmedabad has been selected as one of the hundred Indian cities to be developed as a smart city under Government of India's flagship Smart Cities Mission.

Calcutta

The City of Joy, Kolkata is perhaps the most culturally rich city in India. Its music, arts, literature are all renowned across the globe. From its food to the accents of its people, Kolkata is a fascinating city to visit in India.

Surat

Surat city was former princely state famous for its food, textile and diamonds. The city ranked 3rd cleanest city of India,4th fastest growing city of the world, selected as the first smart IT city in India, to be developed as a smart city and was awarded best city by the Annual Survey of India.

Gandhinagar

Chennai is the biggest cultural and economic hub in South India, also rated the safest city in India to live. The primary port city of south India ranked 43rd most visited city in the world and also among the most visited Indian cities by foreign tourists.

Proudly retaining its age-old cultural charm, Trivandrum offers a huge variety of sights including incredible museums, beautifully designed palaces, sacred temples and mesmerizing beaches, making the city one of the best tourist spots in South India.

The city is marked by influences of Arabs, Dutch, Phoenicians, Portuguese, Chinese and the British city as well as that of the Indian rule of Chera Dynasty followed by rule of the Feudal Lords. A gaggle of islands inter-connected by ferries, this cosmopolitan town has upmarket stores, art galleries and some of the finest heritage accommodations.
